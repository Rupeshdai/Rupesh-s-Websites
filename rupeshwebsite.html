John Mayer is an American singer-songwriter, guitarist, and record producer. He was born on October 16, 1977, in Bridgeport, Connecticut. Mayer is known for his soulful and bluesy guitar playing and his introspective and often confessional songwriting.

Biography

John Mayer started playing guitar at the age of 13 and was soon performing in local blues clubs. He attended the Berklee College of Music in Boston for a short time before moving to Atlanta, Georgia, where he played in various clubs and gained a following. He signed with Aware Records in 1999 and released his debut album, Room for Squares, in 2001. The album included the hit single "Your Body Is a Wonderland," which won Mayer a Grammy Award for Best Male Pop Vocal Performance. Mayer has since released several more albums and won numerous awards for his music.
